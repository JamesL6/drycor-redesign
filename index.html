Mitigation is the emergency phase — stopping water flow, extracting standing water, placing drying equipment, and preventing secondary damage like mold. Reconstruction is the build-back phase — replacing drywall, flooring, cabinetry, and anything else that was removed or damaged. Most restoration companies hand you off to a contractor after mitigation. We handle both in-house, which means one point of contact and a seamless process.
Structural drying typically takes 3–5 days for standard water damage events. We monitor daily with moisture meters and thermal imaging to track progress and adjust equipment placement as needed. More severe or widespread damage can take longer. We document every reading so your insurance has a complete record of the drying process.
